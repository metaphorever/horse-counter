**Art iterated to final over the gate** (measured each pass with `svgelements`):
1. v1 — head too big (reached x340, ~half the barrel); flat-fill silhouette
   proved forgiving but head/tail overlap deep.
2. v2 — smaller head (x303). Mediums/longs great.
3. v3 — shorter, lower-attaching legs (hooves y≈343, was 378); stockier.
Clover picked the **6-character ("Empire")** minimum; short words clamp to the
6-char body, centered, via `min-width: 6ch`. **Gate passed: "Looks perfect."**

**Key technical resolution:** because all parts share one flat coat fill, they
merge into a single silhouette, so the deep head/tail overlap is forgiving at
every name length — short names settle into a minimum-proportion horse, long names
stretch the belly. The chip *is* the barrel (coat bg + name); head/tail/legs are
positioned layers. Placement math proven in the prototype and recorded in the spec.
